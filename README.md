# Quadruped Robot Dog Mechanical Design

## Overview

This project presents the mechanical design and structural development of a quadruped robotic dog created using Tinkercad. The design focuses on achieving a stable four-legged robotic platform that mimics the basic body structure of a real dog while maintaining simplicity, durability, and future expandability.

The project serves as a foundation for future robotic development and can be upgraded with actuators, sensors, control systems, and autonomous navigation technologies.

---

## Project Goals

- Design a mechanically stable quadruped robot.
- Create a balanced body structure with proper weight distribution.
- Develop a practical leg arrangement for future locomotion.
- Improve structural strength and overall stability.
- Establish a platform suitable for future electronic integration.

---

## Mechanical Design Concept

The robot consists of a central body frame connected to four legs positioned symmetrically around the chassis. The design emphasizes stability and balance while maintaining a lightweight structure.

Special consideration was given to:

- Body dimensions and proportions.
- Leg placement and support geometry.
- Structural rigidity.
- Center of gravity positioning.
- Ease of future assembly and modification.

---

## Design Features

### Stable Body Structure
The main chassis is designed to provide sufficient support while maintaining a compact and lightweight profile.

### Symmetrical Leg Arrangement
The four-leg configuration improves balance and provides a solid foundation for future walking mechanisms.

### Modular Architecture
The design allows future integration of:

- Servo motors
- Sensors
- Microcontrollers
- Communication modules

### Future Expandability
The structure was designed with future robotic enhancements in mind.
